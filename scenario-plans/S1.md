## Part 1 — Scenario narrative

### Organisation
**YAT College** — a mid-size Australian vocational college (a registered training organisation). It runs core
business systems it is progressively moving to AWS with help from an external consultancy. It is expanding to
serve an offshore student base in **India** (a GIFT City / offshore campus driver), which introduces data
residency and regulatory pressure.

### People
- **Sam Walker — YAT ICT Manager.** The persistent client-side acceptance authority across every engagement;
  receives presentations, gives sign-offs and project closure.
- **Pat Lin — MTS Senior Consultant.** Leads the consultancy's delivery.
- **MTS** — the cloud consultancy YAT engages; delivers via named engagements and (in CL3) a four-person
  improvement team with a rotating lead.
- **YAT senior management** — approve major proposals.

### Systems
Three systems rotate through assessment and practice roles (the no-leakage invariant; see
[scenario-flow.md](../../docs/scenario-flow.md)): the **LMS** (CL1 assessment / CL2 practice), the public
**Website** (CL2 assessment / CL3 practice), and the **Ledgerline** accounting system (CL3 assessment / CL1
practice). Each advances through fixed states (on-prem → single-AZ → HA); a completed project persists into
later states.

### Situation & drivers
MTS is engaged to (CL1) migrate the LMS to a cloud foundation and harden it for high availability; (CL2) design
the website's global expansion with disaster recovery and a data-residency-aware microservice; (CL3) improve
the Ledgerline accounting system from its single-AZ baseline under Indian regulatory constraints. Each is a
real consulting engagement with a brief, requirements, stakeholders and sign-off gates.

### Constraints & tone
In-world only — every artefact reads as a real document a YAT staffer or MTS consultant would find on the
intranet (sole exception: the UoC footer on migrated docs). Indian regulation (CERT-In logging; Companies-Act
books-of-account) and data residency drive the CL2/CL3 compliance work. Tone: professional consulting
engagement, plausible and document-rich.
