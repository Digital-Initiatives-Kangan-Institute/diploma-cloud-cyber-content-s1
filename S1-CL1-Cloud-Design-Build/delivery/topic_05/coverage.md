## What this Topic must cover

Turning the **completed written business case** (Topics 2–4) into a board presentation and **delivering it for sign-off** — the communication half of AT1, assessed at the **Part B presentation event**. The content is already built; this Topic is about communicating and defending it. Three components, tracking the phases of the event:

- **C1 — From document to pitch (prepare & rehearse).** Distil the written business case into a board deck (on the YAT Board Presentation Deck template), assemble what's tabled to the board (the evaluation, the action plan), and — emphasised — **rehearse the delivery**: practising the walkthrough, timing it to the window, and anticipating the board's questions before facing the room.
- **C2 — Deliver the case.** Present to the board: report the case, walk through the evaluation, and put the ask — in plain English pitched to a non-technical audience.
- **C3 — Handle the room.** Q&A, feedback and sign-off: seek and respond to feedback, use listening & questioning, secure the board's sign-off — and defend the analysis/knowledge the assessor probes.

## 2. AT1 equivalence / alignment

| AT1 element | How Topic 5 aligns |
|---|---|
| **Part B — Board Presentation** (observed event; criteria **B1–B9**) | Direct — the whole Topic prepares Part B: build the pitch (C1 → B2/B3/B4), deliver it (C2 → B1/B2/B3/B4/B7/B9), handle Q&A + secure sign-off (C3 → B5/B6/B8/B9). This event evidences the presentation UoC for all three units. |

**Format facts (from the AT1 assessor doc, to mirror in the practice activity):** deck of **8–10 slides + speaker notes** on the YAT Board Presentation Deck template; **10–15 min present + ~5 min board Q&A/feedback/sign-off**; board role-played (Sam Walker — YAT ICT Manager; Pat Lin — MTS Senior Consultant; a peer); the ask = approval of the action plan (BC §10); feedback captured + sign-off obtained.

**Practice-activity alignment:** students build and deliver a **board presentation of their practice business case** (YAT Accounting System / Ledgerline) to a mock board, then field Q&A — mirroring AT1 Part B against a different system. The BC they present is the one completed across Topics 2–4.
