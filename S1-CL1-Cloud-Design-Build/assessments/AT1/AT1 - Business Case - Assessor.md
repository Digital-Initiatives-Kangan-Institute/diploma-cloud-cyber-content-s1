## Teacher/Assessor instructions

### Assessment overview

Students are assessed on the production and presentation of a **Business Case for the YAT LMS Cloud Migration**. The assessment is split into two parts (Parts A and B — see *Task(s) to be assessed* below):

- **Part A (written):** the Business Case document, including all four appendices (CBA detail, Knowledge Evidence responses, supporting research, Feedback Record)
- **Part B (observed):** the Business Case presentation delivered to the role-played YAT board, with sign-off captured from the role-played YAT ICT Manager

This is an **open-book** assessment. Students may use the YAT intranet (https://www.placeholder.com.au), the AWS Pricing Calculator, AWS Academy lab environments, course reference materials, and external research (which must be cited) throughout. Where the Business Case requires synthesis (e.g. the current-state summary in §4), students must produce content in their own words — verbatim reproduction of intranet material is not satisfactory.

**Reasonable adjustment** for this assessment may include extending the time allowed, varying the location or format of the presentation event (e.g. in-person vs video conference), allowing the student to present to a recorded audience for asynchronous assessment where appropriate, or providing one-on-one verbal explanation of board questions where needed.

**Teacher/assessor support level:** the teacher/assessor may clarify task requirements and the scenario but must not guide the student to specific recommendations or correct answers. During the presentation, the teacher/assessor (in the role of the YAT ICT Manager) may ask substantive questions but must not coach the student through their responses.

**Submission:** Part A (Business Case) is submitted via the LMS as a `.docx` with all four appendices populated. Part B (presentation deck) is submitted via the LMS as a `.pptx`. The completed Feedback Record and the signed Sign-off block are attached to Part A.

The assessment will not proceed if for any reason it is not safe to do so. You must advise the student of the reason for suspending the assessment, and what safety action should be taken. Advise the student of revised arrangements for the assessment when it is safe to do so.

There is a zero tolerance for plagiarism, cheating and collusion. Students will be expected to make a declaration that all work is their own prior to submission. Refer to the Training and Assessment Policy for further information.

### Task(s) to be assessed

> All scenario materials, organisational policies, templates, reference documents, and previous-project business cases for YAT College are available on the **YAT intranet** at **https://www.placeholder.com.au** — students sign in to the intranet at the start of the engagement and refer to it throughout.

In this project, students take on the role of a consultant from **MP Tech Solutions (MTS)** engaged by YAT College to lead a migration of their mission-critical Learning Management System (LMS) to the cloud. AT1 is the **analysis and planning** phase of that engagement — students produce a Business Case for the migration and present it to the YAT board for approval of the action plan.

There are **two parts** to this assessment (Parts A and B). Each part is individually assessed as Satisfactory / Not Yet Satisfactory.

#### Part A — Business Case

Students produce a written Business Case for the YAT LMS Cloud Migration using YAT's standard Business Case template.

The completed Business Case must cover:

- **§1 Executive Summary** — one-page summary of the recommendation
- **§2 Engagement context** — who the student is (MTS consultant), the engagement, the decision being asked of the board
- **§3 Strategic Alignment Analysis** — analyse YAT's ICT Strategic Plan against the industry environment and organisational objectives
- **§4 Current State of YAT's ICT** — synthesised summary from YAT intranet materials (synthesis, not verbatim reproduction)
- **§5 Gap Analysis** — gaps between strategic objectives and current state, improvement opportunities, proposed changes
- **§6 Options Considered and Evaluation** — workload definition, options assessed (in-house renewal vs cloud migration to AWS), initial impact and difficulty assessment
- **§7 Cost-Benefit Analysis** — full 5-year CBA, assumptions, summary tables, avoided-downtime benefit, comparison summary, Year-1 cash-flow, sensitivity analysis
- **§8 Risk and Impact Assessment** — intangibles comparison + risk register for the recommended option
- **§9 Recommendation** — chosen option with rationale
- **§10 Action Plan** — prioritised changes, implementation schedule, standards / targets / success metrics, implementation methods, alignment with YAT's change-management procedure
- **§11 Next Steps and Decision Requested** — what the board is being asked to approve
- **Sign-off block** — completed during/after the presentation
- **Appendix 1 — CBA detailed line items**
- **Appendix 2 — Knowledge Evidence** — short-answer responses linking the student's work to underlying cloud and evaluation principles
- **Appendix 3 — Supporting Research** — AWS Pricing Calculator export, citations
- **Appendix 4 — Feedback Record** — completed during/after the presentation event

**Resources available on the YAT intranet:**

- The YAT Business Case template (download from the intranet's **Templates** section)
- The YAT Feedback Record template (download from the intranet's **Templates** section)
- The YAT scenario materials — strategic plan, current ICT environment description, organisational policies, role brief, ICT manager consultation notes, reference materials
- The YAT **Document Archive** containing examples of previous business cases produced by MTS for YAT — students should review at least one example before starting their own as a reference for what a YAT business case looks like

**External resources students will need:**

- AWS Pricing Calculator (calculator.aws) — to research the AWS cost line items for the cloud option
- AWS Academy Cloud Foundations [104469] + AWS Academy Cloud Architecting [172221] — authorised lab environments

**Part A is submitted to the LMS as the populated Business Case (`.docx`) with all four appendices completed.**

#### Part B — Presentation to the YAT board

Students prepare a slide deck and **present their Business Case to the YAT board** to seek approval of the action plan in Business Case §10.

The presentation deck is built using the YAT Board Presentation Deck template (downloadable from the intranet's **Templates** section). The deck is 8–10 slides plus speaker notes; each slide walks the board through one section of the Business Case.

Students should also review at least one example of a previous board presentation deck from the YAT **Document Archive** on the intranet — these accompany the previous business cases referenced for Part A — as a reference for what a YAT board presentation looks like in style, depth, and length.

The **presentation event** is:

- **Duration:** 10–15 minutes of presenting + 5 minutes of board questions, feedback, and sign-off
- **Audience:** the YAT board — role-played by the teacher/assessor (as Sam Walker, YAT ICT Manager, and/or Pat Lin, MTS Senior Consultant) and a peer (as a board member)
- **Required artefacts at the event:**
  - The completed Business Case submitted to the board at least 48 hours in advance
  - The presentation deck with populated speaker notes
  - A printed or shared **Feedback Record** ready for the student to populate during/after the meeting (using the YAT Feedback Record template)
  - A printed or shared **sign-off sheet** for the board to sign at the end (the Sign-off block from the Business Case)

During the event the student:

- Walks the board through the Business Case using the deck
- Answers the board's questions during Q&A (the assessor's questions probe both the analysis and the student's underlying knowledge of cloud and evaluation principles)
- Captures board feedback in the Feedback Record
- Obtains the board's sign-off on the action plan in Business Case §10

**Part B is observed by the teacher/assessor against the Presentation Marking Guide (later in this document). The completed Feedback Record and the signed Sign-off block are attached to the Business Case (as Appendix 4 and the Sign-off block respectively) when the bundle is submitted to the LMS.**

- **Per-Part NYS does not require resubmission of the other Part.** If Part A is Satisfactory but Part B is NYS, the student resubmits only Part B (re-deliver the presentation event).
- **Assessor comments are expected for any NYS** to support the student's resubmission.
