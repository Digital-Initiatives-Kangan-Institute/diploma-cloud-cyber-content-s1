## 1. About this assessment

This is the first of three assessment tasks in the **S1-CL1 Cloud Design and Build** cluster. Across this cluster you take on the role of a consultant from MP Tech Solutions (MTS) engaged by YAT College to lead a migration of their mission-critical Learning Management System (LMS) to the cloud. AT1 is the **analysis and planning** phase of that engagement.

In AT1 you will:

1. **Prepare a Business Case** for the LMS Cloud Migration — using the supplied Business Case Template — covering strategic alignment analysis, current-state synthesis, gap analysis, options/evaluation, a full 5-year Cost-Benefit Analysis, a risk and impact assessment, your recommendation, the action plan you propose, and a Knowledge Evidence appendix.
2. **Prepare and deliver a presentation of your Business Case** to the YAT board (role-played by your assessor + a peer) — using the supplied Board Presentation Deck Template — answer board questions, capture feedback in the Feedback Record, and obtain sign-off on the action plan.
3. **Submit** the Business Case (with all appendices populated) and the deck to the LMS.

The work you produce in AT1 flows into AT2 and AT3 of this cluster. The action plan approved at the end of AT1 becomes the brief for the build work in AT2.

## 2. The scenario

YAT College is a Registered Training Organisation (RTO) based at 175 Cremorne Street, Cremorne VIC, with around 60 staff and 800 students. Their mission-critical LMS — running on a single on-premises Windows Server 2016 / MySQL / DOODLE stack — is at the end of its hardware life and falls short of YAT's strategic availability target of 99.9%. YAT's ICT staff are highly capable on their current environment but lack cloud experience, which is why MTS has been engaged.

The full scenario — strategic plan, current ICT environment, organisational policies, role brief, consultation notes with the YAT ICT Manager, and supporting reference materials — is available on the **YAT intranet** (the scenario site). You will sign in to the intranet at the start of the engagement and refer to it throughout.

**Your role on this engagement:** You are an MTS consultant reporting to **Pat Lin** (MTS Senior Consultant). Pat liaises with **Sam Walker** (YAT ICT Manager) for sign-offs. Sam will receive your Business Case and is your primary YAT-side stakeholder.

## 3. What you will do — the three tasks

### 3.1 Prepare the Business Case

Using the **Business Case Template** (`business-case-template-S1-CL1-AT1`), populate every section. The Business Case is the primary written deliverable and contains:

- **§1 Executive Summary** — a one-page summary of your recommendation (write last)
- **§2 Engagement context** — who you are, the engagement, the decision being asked of the board
- **§3 Strategic Alignment Analysis** — analyse YAT's ICT Strategic Plan against the industry environment and organisational objectives
- **§4 Current State of YAT's ICT** — synthesised summary from the intranet materials (synthesis, not verbatim reproduction)
- **§5 Gap Analysis** — gaps, improvement opportunities, proposed changes
- **§6 Options Considered and Evaluation** — workload definition, options assessed (in-house renewal vs cloud migration to AWS), initial impact and difficulty assessment
- **§7 Cost-Benefit Analysis** — assumptions, summary tables for both options, avoided-downtime benefit, comparison summary, Year-1 cash-flow comparison, sensitivity analysis. The detailed line items live in Appendix 1 of the same document.
- **§8 Risk and Impact Assessment** — intangibles comparison and risk register for the recommended option
- **§9 Recommendation** — your chosen option with rationale
- **§10 Action Plan** — prioritised changes, implementation schedule, standards / targets / success metrics, implementation methods, alignment with YAT's change-management procedure, risk register
- **§11 Next Steps and Decision Requested** — what you are asking the board to decide today
- **Sign-off block** — completed during/after your presentation
- **Appendix 1 — CBA detailed line items** — every cost line you researched and computed
- **Appendix 2 — Knowledge Evidence** — short-answer responses linking your work to underlying cloud and evaluation principles
- **Appendix 3 — Supporting Research** — AWS Pricing Calculator export, citations
- **Appendix 4 — Feedback Record** — completed using the supplied Feedback Record template during/after the presentation

You will need to **research AWS pricing yourself** using the AWS Pricing Calculator (see Appendix 1 of the Business Case Template for the structure your AWS line items must follow). YAT-internal cost figures for the on-prem option are provided in the scenario materials.

### 3.2 Prepare and deliver the presentation

Using the **Board Presentation Deck Template** (`board-presentation-deck-template-S1-CL1-AT1`), prepare a deck that walks the YAT board through your Business Case. The deck is 8–10 slides plus speaker notes.

You will deliver the presentation to the **YAT board**, role-played by your assessor (as Sam Walker / Pat Lin) and a peer (as a board member). The presentation event is:

- **Duration:** 10–15 minutes of presenting + 5 minutes of board questions, feedback, and sign-off
- **Format:** in-person on campus or via video conference (confirm with your assessor)
- **Required artefacts at the event:** completed Business Case submitted to the board at least 48 hours in advance; the deck; a printed or shared **Feedback Record template** ready to be populated; a printed or shared sign-off sheet for the board to sign at the end (the Sign-off block in the Business Case)

You will:
- Walk the board through your Business Case using the deck
- Answer the board's questions during Q&A
- Capture board feedback in the Feedback Record
- Obtain sign-off on the action plan

The presentation is **observed**. Your assessor will mark you against the Presentation Observation Rubric (every criterion in that rubric is tagged to a specific UoC requirement — see the rubric for what is being assessed).

### 3.3 Submit

After the presentation event, submit the following to the LMS:

- The completed Business Case (`.docx`), with all appendices populated:
  - Appendix 1 — CBA detail
  - Appendix 2 — Knowledge Evidence responses
  - Appendix 3 — Supporting research (AWS Pricing Calculator export attached)
  - Appendix 4 — completed Feedback Record from the presentation event
- The Business Case Sign-off block completed and signed by the role-played superior
- The presentation deck (`.pptx`)

## 8. Tips for success

- **Start with the scenario.** Read the YAT intranet end-to-end before you start writing — particularly the ICT Strategic Plan, the current ICT environment, the role brief, and the ICT manager consultation notes.
- **Synthesise, don't copy.** Section 4 (Current State) is the section most likely to drift into verbatim reproduction. Write it in your own words and focus on what's material to the migration.
- **Show your AWS pricing working.** Generate an AWS Pricing Calculator estimate, export it, and attach it as Appendix 3. Numbers without evidence are not credible.
- **Sense-check your CBA totals.** Year-1 outlay differences matter to senior management even when 5-year totals favour one option — make the cash-flow position visible.
- **Be honest in §8 Intangibles.** A one-sided intangibles table — only positives for your preferred option — is less credible than an honest trade-off discussion.
- **The action plan is what the board approves.** §10 is the most scrutinised section. Spend time on prioritisation, scheduling, and alignment with YAT's change-management procedure.
- **Prepare for the Q&A.** The assessor will probe both the analysis (PC-evidencing) and your underlying knowledge (KE-probing). The Q&A is when your speaker preparation pays off.
- **Bring the sign-off sheet to the meeting.** Sign-off is part of the assessment — you cannot achieve Satisfactory without it.
