## How to use this list

When an assessment task or design decision touches on "legislative requirements" or "compliance obligations", students should reference the items below by name and identify which one applies. Where a YAT policy (Privacy Policy, Backup and Retention Policy, etc.) already operationalises a legislative requirement, students should cite both the policy and the underlying legislation.

## Privacy and data protection

- **Privacy Act 1988 (Cth)** — federal privacy legislation; sets the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) framework. YAT, as an organisation holding student personal information, is bound by the APPs. Operationalised at YAT in `internal-privacy-policy.md`.
- **Australian Privacy Principles (APPs)** — 13 principles inside the Privacy Act covering collection, use, disclosure, quality, security, access, and cross-border transfer. APP 8 (cross-border disclosure) underpins YAT's Australian-data-residency requirement for the migrated LMS.
- **Notifiable Data Breaches scheme (Privacy Act Part IIIC)** — mandatory notification to affected individuals and the OAIC where an eligible data breach is likely to result in serious harm. Operationalised at YAT in `internal-security-and-incident-response.md` §5.

## RTO regulation

- **National Vocational Education and Training Regulator Act 2011 (Cth)** — established ASQA (Australian Skills Quality Authority) as the national regulator for VET.
- **Standards for Registered Training Organisations (RTOs) 2015** — the substantive ASQA standards YAT operates under. Sets requirements around training and assessment, learner support, governance, records and reporting. Drives the long student-records retention periods in `internal-backup-retention-policy.md`.
- **AVETMISS data reporting** — statutory data reporting to ASQA / DET via the Total VET Activity (TVA) reporting framework. Referenced in `internal-lms-application-spec-S1-CL1-AT1.md`.

## Workplace health and safety

- **Occupational Health and Safety Act 2004 (Vic)** — Victorian WHS legislation; YAT's primary workplace safety framework. Operationalised at YAT in `internal-whs-policy.md`.
- **Occupational Health and Safety Regulations 2017 (Vic)** — subordinate regulations under the OHS Act.

## Anti-discrimination and accessibility

- **Disability Discrimination Act 1992 (Cth)** — federal anti-discrimination law; relevant to accessibility of YAT's services, including digital services (the LMS). Drives the WCAG 2.1 AA requirement in the LMS application specification.
- **Equal Opportunity Act 2010 (Vic)** — Victorian anti-discrimination law; covers employment and provision of services.

## Employment and financial records

- **Fair Work Act 2009 (Cth)** — federal employment legislation; sets record-keeping obligations that drive the 7-year retention for staff employment records in `internal-backup-retention-policy.md`.
- **Income Tax Assessment Act 1936 (Cth) — record-keeping provisions** — drives the 7-year retention for financial records.
- **Superannuation Guarantee (Administration) Act 1992 (Cth)** — superannuation record-keeping obligations for staff.

## Online safety and student protection

- **Online Safety Act 2021 (Cth)** — federal online-safety framework; relevant to YAT's responsibilities for online learning environments including the LMS.
- **Child Wellbeing and Safety Act 2005 (Vic)** — Victorian child-safety framework; relevant to YAT students under 18.

## Intellectual property

- **Copyright Act 1968 (Cth)** — federal copyright law. Relevant to YAT's use and distribution of course materials (statutory educational licence under Part VB applies to RTOs).

## Cyber security and critical infrastructure

- **Security of Critical Infrastructure Act 2018 (Cth)** — federal critical-infrastructure framework. Not currently applicable to private RTOs but worth tracking; relevant if YAT's footprint or function changes materially.

## Notes

- Citations above are by short title only. Full Commonwealth Acts are available at [legislation.gov.au](https://www.legislation.gov.au); Victorian Acts at [legislation.vic.gov.au](https://www.legislation.vic.gov.au).
- Legislation versions referenced are current as at the time of writing (2026); always confirm the in-force version when making a real compliance call.
- Students undertaking the cluster ATs should treat this as a starting point — not a complete compliance map. Specialised work (e.g. clinical health data, financial services, defence) would introduce additional regimes not in scope here.
